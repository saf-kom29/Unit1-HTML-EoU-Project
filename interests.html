My Interests & Hobbies

I have many hobbies that are independent of computer science and coding-- interests that help me stay motivated, balanced, and content with myself. Here's a list of a few!

Drawing/Sketching

Whenever I feel like it, I always love sitting down with a pencil and a sketchbook and doodling small things. Usually, I focus on characters from various animated media and OCs (Original Characters). It helps me feel happy and productive, and allows me to channel creativity and imagination in my usually logic-based life.

A list of the things I draw the most:
  • Flowers and plants: usually roses, dahlias, mushroom variaties, and lotuses.
  • Characters from other media: I'm flexible and often try to be diverse with this, but my top favorites to draw are characters from Sonic the Hedgehog, JJK, and TADC.
  • Original Characters: Not much explanation needed. I really like creating new characters and drawing them as much as I can. That's where the creativity comes from.

What I like about drawing: Creative outlet, very abstract and imaginative, requires thinking and strong visualizing skills.


Writing

Writing has always been a source of comfort and inspiration for me. Much like drawing, I enjoy writing because I love telling my own stories and it allows my imagination to run free.

Genres I enjoy writing:

  • Psychological Horror
  • Sci-Fi
  • Fantasy

Personally, I think writing is essential to me succeeding in multiple fields, as I find it the most accessible and easiest form of media for me to express myself in. Writing can also develop critical thinking and planning skills, as to compose a story you have to account for multiple aspects of your characters, settings, etc.

Gaming

As you can tell by the pictures, I also like playing various video games during my free time (although nowadays, not very often). It's an easy stress-reliever and provides multiple unique experiences when playing the right games.

A few of my favorites are, but are not limited to:

  1. Terraria: an immersive 2D sandbox game where you can craft thousands of weapons, build unique architecture, and house NPCS.
  2. Undertale: a 2D RPG game, one that teaches you the values of kindness, determination, and the consequences of your actions.
  3. Sonic the Hedgehog: although the STH franchise is expansive and not limited to one game, I enjoy the timing-based platforming experience Sonic games bring.

I enjoy gaming because of the thousands of different experiences they bring, the unpredictability of new games, and the development of cognative and finger reflexes.


How all My Hobbbies Connect to CS

In general, it's important to have a diverse palette of hobbies and talents, but you should have a main focus point that most (if not all) of your hobbies tie back to. For me, gaming inspired me to find out how all of the encoded mechanics and sprites work and what makes them behave the way they do. Writing and drawing helped me discover the creativity to use my developing coding skills to create new things and find inspiration in others' work.
